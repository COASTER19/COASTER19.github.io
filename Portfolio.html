Room Automation System

Using an Arduino with a bunch of relays, I built a room automation system capable of remotely controlling the lights, projector screen, and door lock. The system also has a webcam to remotely monitor the room. Boxes with 4 outlets each control miscelaneous devices. The website was built following tutorials from W3Schools, and is also the basis for this website.

FPGA Robot Arm

The goal of this project was to project image and text notifications on a desk and interact with that projection via computer vision. The physical hardware platform for the project was a robotic arm, disguised as a common desk lamp, with a camera and projector at the end, all controlled by an FPGA. Notifications come from from a connected computer and are keystone-corrected when projected, to eliminate distortion caused by projecting at an angle

Hall-wide audio system

One of my halls oldest traditions is an audio system in all the bathrooms, lounges, and kitchen. After more than 10 years of continuous use, however, it was in bad condition. When the amp broke that was the final straw for a complete overhaul of the system. Over the next year (using parts from MIT's e-waste), I rebuilt the entire system, replacing the speakers, adding attenuators to control the volume in different rooms, and replaced much of the wiring which was falling apart. It is still in use today.
